Chicken Biriyani

Ingredients:

  • Chicken pieces
  • Basmati rice
  • Onions (sliced)
  • Tomatoes (chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Yogurt
  • Biryani masala (or a blend of spices like cumin, coriander, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon)
  • Red chili powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Cashews and raisins (optional)
  • Water
  • Salt

Instructions:

Marinate the Chicken:

In a bowl, mix chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, salt, and some chopped mint and coriander leaves. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes to absorb the flavors.

Prepare the Rice:

Wash and soak basmati rice for about 30 minutes. In a large pot, bring water to a boil, add salt, and cook the rice until it's 70-80% cooked. Drain the water.

Cook the Chicken:

In a separate pan, heat ghee or oil. Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Add tomatoes and cook until they soften. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is partially cooked.

Layering:

In a heavy-bottomed pan or biryani pot, layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken. Sprinkle chopped mint, coriander leaves, fried onions, and a bit of ghee on top of the rice.

Dum Cooking:

Seal the pot with a tight-fitting lid or aluminum foil to trap the steam. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes (dum cooking) to allow the flavors to infuse and the chicken and rice to cook completely.

Final Touch:

Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Optionally, garnish with fried cashews and raisins and a drizzle of lemon juice.

Serve:

Serve the chicken biryani hot with raita or a side salad.

Nutrients:

Proteins, Carbohydrates, Fats, Vitamins and Minerals
